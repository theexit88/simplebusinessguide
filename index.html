📚 Why This Comes First

Your business name must be legally "distinguishable" from all existing entities registered in your state. If someone already has your name, you cannot use it — even if you've been operating informally. Check this before buying a domain, printing cards, or building a logo.

Note: a state-level name registration does not protect your name nationally. That requires a federal trademark (see Step 15). But state availability is the minimum required to form your entity.

⚠️ Name Rules to Know

Your name must include a designator like LLC, L.L.C., Inc., Corp., or Co. — your state will tell you which are accepted. Names are compared without regard to punctuation or common words like "The" — so "The Blue Door LLC" and "Blue Door Inc" may conflict. When in doubt, pick a distinctive name.

📚 State Registration ≠ Trademark Protection

Registering your LLC in Indiana does not stop a business in California from using the same name. A federal trademark gives you exclusive nationwide rights to a name or logo in your industry category. If someone else already holds a trademark similar to your chosen name, you risk a cease-and-desist letter — even after building your brand. Search now, before investing anything.

You don't need to file a trademark yet — just verify no conflicts exist. See Step 15 for when and how to file your own.

🔧 USPTO TESS — Free Trademark Search

Go to uspto.gov/trademarks/search and search your exact name AND phonetically similar names. A conflict doesn't need to be identical — it must only be "confusingly similar" in the same industry class.

✅ Recommendation for Most SMB Owners — LLC + S-Corp Election

Form an LLC for the legal structure (simple, flexible, strong liability protection). Then elect S-Corp tax treatment via IRS Form 2553 once your net profit consistently exceeds ~$50,000/year.

Here's why the S-Corp election saves real money:

  • Default LLC: $120K profit → ~$18,400 in self-employment tax (15.3%)
  • LLC + S-Corp: Pay yourself $60K salary → ~$9,200 SE tax on salary. The remaining $60K in owner distributions has zero SE tax.
  • Annual savings: ~$9,200. The extra compliance costs (payroll service ~$600/yr, CPA ~$500/yr more) are far outweighed.

⏰ Critical deadline: IRS Form 2553 must be filed within 75 days of forming your LLC to take effect the same tax year. See Step 9 for the full S-Corp election process.

⚠️ Avoid C-Corp Unless You're Raising Venture Capital

C-Corps are taxed twice — the company pays 21% corporate income tax, and then you pay personal income tax on dividends taken out. This structure is designed for companies seeking institutional investors or planning to go public. For a local or regional SMB, it creates unnecessary tax complexity and cost.

📚 LLC Governance: Member-Managed vs. Manager-Managed

When you form an LLC, you'll choose a management structure. Member-managed means all owners (members) participate in running the business — best for small teams where everyone is active. Manager-managed means you designate one or more managers (who may or may not be members) to run day-to-day operations — better if you have passive investors or a silent partner.

For a solo founder or small active team, member-managed is standard.

Choose Your Registered Agent 📚 Education 💰 $0–$125/yr

📚 What is a Registered Agent?

Every U.S. business entity must designate a Registered Agent — a person or company with a physical street address in your state who is available during all business hours to receive legal documents on behalf of your business. These include lawsuits (service of process), government compliance notices, tax correspondence, and state filing reminders.

Why not just use your own home address?

  • Your home address becomes permanent public record — searchable by anyone, including data brokers.
  • You could be served with a lawsuit in front of employees, clients, or family.
  • You must be physically present at the address during all business hours, 365 days a year.
  • Missing a legal notice can result in a default judgment against you.
🛠 DIY Path — Acting as Your Own Registered Agent

You can legally serve as your own registered agent in most states if you have a physical street address (no P.O. boxes) in the state of formation and can be present there during all normal business hours.

Cost: $0

Downsides: Your personal address becomes public record. You must be reachable at that address at all times during business hours. If you move, you must update the state immediately. You'll manage all compliance deadlines yourself.

This is only recommended for businesses operating out of a commercial office address — never your home.

📚 What is an Operating Agreement?

An Operating Agreement is the LLC's governing document. It defines: ownership percentages, how profits and losses are split, how decisions are made, what happens if a member wants to leave, and how the business is dissolved. Even for a single-member LLC it is critical because:

  • It reinforces the "corporate veil" — demonstrating your LLC is legally separate from you personally. Without it, a court may disregard your LLC and go after your personal assets.
  • Banks require it to open a business bank account.
  • Some states (California, New York, Missouri, Maine, Delaware) require it by law.
  • It protects you in partner disputes — without one, your state's default rules apply, which may not reflect what you intended.
🛠 DIY Path

Free templates are available from several reputable sources:

  • Rocket Lawyer — Free basic template with a guided Q&A. Good starting point for single-member LLCs.
  • LegalZoom — Free template library. Requires creating an account.
  • Your state's Secretary of State website — Some states provide official sample operating agreements.

Cost: $0 for template. For multi-member LLCs, strongly consider an attorney review ($300–$800 one-time) — money well spent to prevent future partner disputes.

📚 Articles of Organization vs. Articles of Incorporation

Articles of Organization = the LLC formation document. Establishes entity name, registered agent, management structure, and business purpose.

Articles of Incorporation = the Corporation formation document (S-Corp or C-Corp). Also includes authorized share structure, initial directors, and par value of shares.

⏰ Two federal deadlines begin the day you file:

  • Your chosen business name (confirmed available)

  • Business address and management structure choice
  • A credit card for the state filing fee

Typical processing: 1–3 business days online; 1–3 weeks by mail.

Must file within 75 days of formation to take effect this tax year. Don't miss this window.
▾
📚 How the S-Corp Election Works

By default, a single-member LLC is taxed as a sole proprietor — all net profit is subject to self-employment tax (15.3% up to ~$168K, then 2.9%). Filing Form 2553 tells the IRS to treat your LLC as an S-Corporation for tax purposes only — your legal structure remains an LLC.

With S-Corp status, you split income into two buckets:

  • Reasonable salary — W-2 wages you pay yourself. Subject to payroll/SE tax.
  • Owner distributions — Profits above salary passed to you. No SE tax.

The IRS requires your salary to be "reasonable" for your role. Typically set salary at 40–60% of total income and take the rest as distributions. A CPA can help you optimize this split.

⚠️ The 75-Day Deadline

Form 2553 must be filed within 75 days of your LLC formation date to be effective for the current tax year. After that window, you must wait until the following January 1. Set a calendar reminder the day you file your Articles. Missing this can cost you thousands in unnecessary SE taxes for an entire year.

Alternatively, file by March 15 to take effect for the next full tax year.

🛠 DIY Path

Download IRS Form 2553 directly from irs.gov — it's free. The form requires your EIN (see Step 10), LLC formation date, and tax year information. Mail or fax to your IRS service center (listed in the form instructions).

Tip: Send via certified mail and keep the receipt — the IRS does not send immediate confirmation. Confirmation letter typically arrives 60–90 days later.

Cost: $0

🏢 Professional Path

Northwest Registered Agent offers S-Corp election filing as an add-on service. Your CPA or business attorney can also prepare and file Form 2553 — typically $100–$200 as a standalone service. Given the tax savings at stake, having a professional handle this is worth the small fee to ensure it's filed correctly and on time.

✅ When to Make This Election

Elect S-Corp status when your business consistently nets $50,000+ per year after expenses. Below that threshold, the additional compliance costs (payroll service ~$600/yr, additional CPA fees ~$500/yr) may outweigh the SE tax savings. Consult a CPA to confirm the right timing for your specific income level.

File Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) Report ⚠️ 90-Day Federal Deadline

📚 What is a BOI Report?

The Corporate Transparency Act requires most U.S. LLCs and corporations to report their beneficial owners (people who own 25%+ or exercise substantial control) to FinCEN — the Financial Crimes Enforcement Network. This is a federal anti-money-laundering measure.

Businesses formed in 2024 or later must file within 90 days of formation. Failure to file can result in civil penalties up to $591/day and criminal penalties.

🛠 DIY Path — FinCEN Direct Filing

File free directly at boiefiling.fincen.gov. You'll need: your EIN, LLC formation date, business address, and personal information for each beneficial owner (legal name, date of birth, address, ID document). Takes approximately 15–20 minutes. Cost: $0

🏢 Professional Path

Northwest Registered Agent offers BOI report filing as an add-on service for clients, ensuring the report is filed correctly and on time. Typical third-party fee: $25–$75. Given the steep daily penalty for non-filing, having this handled professionally is worth the small cost.

⚠️ Check Current Requirements

BOI reporting has been subject to legal challenges since its introduction. Always verify the current filing status at fincen.gov/boi before filing — requirements may have been updated.

An EIN is a 9-digit federal tax ID number — essentially your business's Social Security Number. Open a Dedicated Business Bank Account ⚠️ Do Not Skip

📚 The Corporate Veil — Why This Is Non-Negotiable

The entire purpose of an LLC is to separate you legally from your business. If you mix personal and business finances — known as "commingling" — a court can pierce the corporate veil, making your personal savings, home, and assets fair game in any lawsuit against your business. This is the single most common mistake that destroys LLC protection.

Do not accept a single dollar of business revenue into your personal account. Open the business account before you begin transacting.

  • Certified Articles of Organization (from Step 8)
  • Operating Agreement (from Step 7)
  • Government-issued photo ID
  • Initial deposit (Mercury: $0; most banks: $25–$100)

Recommended banks: Mercury (online, free, startup-friendly), Relay Financial (great for expense management), Chase Business Complete, your local credit union.

📚 Trademark vs. Business Registration — Important Distinction

Registering your LLC protects your name within your state for entity registration purposes only. It does not stop a business in another state from using a similar name. A federal trademark gives you exclusive nationwide rights to use your business name, logo, or slogan in your specific industry category, and allows you to take legal action against infringers anywhere in the U.S.

You checked for conflicts in Step 2. Now is when you consider filing your own mark to create that same protection for others.

Trademark protects: business names, logos, slogans, product names, brand elements
Copyright protects: original creative works — website copy, photography, software, written content, designs (see next step)

✅ When to Prioritize a Trademark

File a trademark if: (1) you're building a consumer brand, (2) you plan to license or franchise, (3) you're in a competitive market where name confusion is a real risk, or (4) your brand name has significant long-term value. For very local service businesses with no expansion plans in the near term, it's lower priority — but never permanently skip it.

🛠 DIY Path — USPTO TEAS Filing

File directly at uspto.gov/trademarks/apply. Use the TEAS Plus application for the lowest fee.

  • TEAS Plus: $250/class — requires selecting a pre-approved description from USPTO's ID Manual
  • TEAS Standard: $350/class — allows custom description; more flexible

Processing takes 8–14 months. You'll receive an application serial number immediately. Your priority date is established the day you file — even before approval.

Tip: Search your industry's "Nice Classification" (the international trademark class system) to file in the right category. Filing in the wrong class offers no protection in your actual industry.

📚 Copyright Basics for Business Owners

Copyright arises automatically the moment you create an original work — your website copy, logo design, photography, software code, written content, videos, and marketing materials are all copyrighted the moment they're created. You don't need to register to own the copyright.

However, registration matters for enforcement:

  • You cannot sue for copyright infringement in federal court without a registration.
  • Registration within 90 days of publication (or before infringement occurs) allows you to claim statutory damages up to $150,000 per work plus attorney's fees — without having to prove actual monetary loss.
  • Registration creates a public record of your ownership, which deters infringement and simplifies licensing negotiations.

What to register: your logo, website content, marketing materials, original photography, proprietary training materials, software, and any creative work that has commercial value to your business.

🛠 DIY Path — U.S. Copyright Office

Register online at copyright.gov/registration.

  • Single work (online form): $45
  • Group of published photos or short online literary works: $65
  • Processing time: 3–11 months for online registration; faster for supplemental registrations

You'll upload a deposit copy of the work during registration. Keep a dated digital record of all original creative assets — useful as supporting evidence if ever challenged.

🏢 Professional Path

For comprehensive intellectual property strategy — especially if you have multiple works, proprietary software, or creative assets that are core to your business — consult an IP attorney. They can bundle multiple registrations, advise on work-for-hire agreements (important if contractors create content for you), and structure your IP ownership properly from the start.

⚠️ Work-for-Hire — Critical for Business Owners

If you hire a freelancer or contractor to create content, design a logo, or write software for your business, copyright belongs to them by default — not you — unless you have a written work-for-hire agreement or copyright assignment clause in your contract. Always include this language in any contract with creative contractors.
